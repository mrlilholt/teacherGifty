Classroom Supply Gifts Teachers Actually Use (From a Teacher)

I’ve been teaching long enough to tell you this with complete honesty: classroom supplies are one of the most genuinely appreciated gifts a teacher can receive. Not because they’re flashy—but because we use them every single day.

Parents often worry that supply gifts feel “boring.” From a teacher’s perspective, they feel thoughtful. They say, “I see what you actually need.”

1. Consumable Supplies (These Never Go to Waste)

Consumables are the safest category of all. They disappear quickly—and that’s a good thing.

  • Pencils & Bulk Classroom Packs – These vanish constantly. A high-quality bulk pencil pack is always welcome.
  • Glue Sticks & Tissues – These are funded out of teachers’ own pockets far more often than parents realize.
  • Sticky Notes – Used for reminders, feedback, and student notes. Teachers love the bright multi-pack sets like these Post-it bundles .

2. Dry Erase Markers (The Real MVP)

If I had to name one supply that teachers never have enough of, it’s dry erase markers. We use them daily—and they dry out faster than anyone expects.

A fresh pack of Expo dry erase markers will get used immediately. No guessing required.

3. Organization Tools Teachers Actually Keep

Organization gifts work best when they’re simple and flexible.

  • Supply Caddies – Perfect for shared tables or centers. A sturdy option like this classroom caddy gets daily use.
  • Drawer Organizers & Bins – Teachers love anything that reduces visual clutter.
  • Label Maker Tape – An underrated favorite among veteran teachers.

4. Tools Teachers Quietly Appreciate

These aren’t glamorous—but they’re lifesavers.

  • Stapler + Hole Punch Set – Teachers use these constantly, and nicer versions matter. A reliable set like this combo often replaces a broken classroom one.

What to Avoid (From a Teacher)

Novelty items are kind—but they often end up stored away. Gifts that take up space, require special storage, or don’t serve a classroom purpose usually don’t get used.

If you’re unsure, supplies are always safer than decor.

The Best Way to Give a Supply Gift

Pair it with a short note: “We noticed how much you do every day—thank you.” Teachers keep those notes far longer than most objects.
